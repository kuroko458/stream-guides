Why Internet Speed Matters

A stable internet connection is one of the most important factors for live streaming. Even if you have a powerful PC and excellent OBS settings, poor internet quality can lead to buffering, dropped frames and blurry streams.

Your upload speed is far more important than your download speed because streaming sends video and audio from your computer to the streaming platform.

Download Speed vs Upload Speed

⬇ Download Speed

Download speed is how fast your device receives data from the internet. It affects:

• Watching YouTube
• Netflix
• Game Downloads
• Web Browsing

⬆ Upload Speed

Upload speed determines how fast your PC sends data to the internet. It affects:

• Live Streaming
• Video Calls
• Cloud Backups
• Uploading Videos

Important Network Terms

⚡ Ping

Ping is the response time between your computer and the server. Lower is better. Recommended: Below 30 ms

📡 Jitter

Jitter measures how consistent your connection is. Recommended: Below 10 ms

📦 Packet Loss

Packet loss means some information never reaches the streaming server. Recommended: 0%

🌍 Latency

Latency is the delay between sending and receiving data. Lower latency gives smoother live streams.

How Much Upload Speed Should You Keep Free?

Never use your full upload bandwidth for streaming. Always keep at least 25% upload speed available for game traffic, Discord, browser usage and network stability.

Example:

  • Upload Speed = 20 Mbps
  • Use around 15 Mbps maximum
  • Leave about 5 Mbps free

This greatly reduces buffering and dropped frames.

How to Improve Upload Speed

🚫 Close Background Apps

Close Steam downloads, OneDrive, Google Drive, Windows Update and other software that uses your upload bandwidth.

🔌 Use Ethernet

A Cat6 or Cat6a Ethernet cable provides a much more stable connection than Wi-Fi.

📡 Upgrade Your Router

Older routers may limit performance. A modern Wi-Fi 6 or Wi-Fi 6E router improves stability and speed.

🌍 Choose the Closest Server

Select the nearest streaming ingest server in OBS whenever possible to reduce latency.

Reduce Ping & Jitter

📍 Use Nearby Servers

Streaming to a nearby server usually gives lower ping and fewer dropped frames.

📴 Reduce Network Traffic

Avoid downloading games, syncing files or streaming videos on other devices while you are live.

🔄 Restart Your Router

Restart your modem and router occasionally to refresh the connection.

📞 Contact Your ISP

If you experience constant packet loss or unstable upload speeds, contact your internet provider.

Common Streaming Internet Problems

🚨 Dropped Frames

Dropped frames usually happen when your upload speed cannot keep up with your selected bitrate.

✔ Lower your bitrate
✔ Use Ethernet
✔ Close background downloads

🐌 Buffering

Viewers experience buffering when your connection becomes unstable.

✔ Keep upload usage below 75%
✔ Reduce resolution if necessary

⚠ High Ping

High ping increases stream delay.

✔ Connect to the nearest server
✔ Restart router
✔ Use wired internet

📦 Packet Loss

Packet loss causes freezing, pixelation and audio glitches.

✔ Check cables
✔ Contact your ISP
✔ Replace faulty router

Advanced Network Tips

🌍 Choose the Closest Ingest Server

Always stream to the nearest YouTube, Twitch, Kick or Loco ingest server for the lowest latency.

⚙ Enable QoS

Quality of Service (QoS) prioritizes OBS traffic over gaming downloads and other devices.

🔒 Secure Your Network

Use WPA2 or WPA3 security to prevent unauthorized users from consuming your bandwidth.

📶 Separate Devices

Move phones, TVs and smart devices to a guest network to reduce congestion.

🔄 Update Router Firmware

Firmware updates improve performance, stability and security.

📈 Monitor Bandwidth

Regularly check your upload speed and network usage before every stream.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much upload speed do I need for 1080p60 streaming?

For stable 1080p60 streaming, aim for at least 15–20 Mbps upload speed. This provides enough bandwidth for your stream while leaving room for other network traffic.

Is download speed important for streaming?

Download speed matters for watching content and downloading games, but upload speed is the most important factor for live streaming because you're sending video and audio to the streaming platform.

Can I stream using Wi-Fi?

Yes, but a wired Ethernet connection is strongly recommended. Ethernet offers lower latency, fewer disconnects, and a more consistent upload speed than Wi-Fi.

What is a good ping for streaming?

A ping below 20 ms is excellent. Between 20–50 ms is generally good. Higher values can increase delay and reduce responsiveness.

What causes dropped frames?

Dropped frames are commonly caused by insufficient upload speed, unstable internet, packet loss, Wi-Fi interference, or streaming with a bitrate that's too high for your connection.

How often should I test my internet?

Test your connection before every stream, especially if you're streaming important events or tournaments.
