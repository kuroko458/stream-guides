Encoder Comparison

AV1

Best video quality with the lowest bitrate. Recommended for RTX 40/50 Series and AMD RX 7000 GPUs.

NVENC H.264

Excellent quality with very low CPU usage. Recommended for most NVIDIA RTX users.

AMD AMF

Hardware encoder for AMD graphics cards. Provides smooth performance with modern GPUs.

x264

CPU-based encoder. Use only if your processor has enough available performance.

OBS Streaming Tips

🎥 Use Hardware Encoding

If you have an NVIDIA RTX GPU, use NVENC AV1 (RTX 40/50 Series) or NVENC H.264 (RTX 20/30 Series). AMD users should use the latest AMF encoder. Hardware encoders reduce CPU usage while maintaining excellent video quality.

🌐 Always Use Ethernet

A wired Ethernet connection is far more stable than Wi-Fi. Packet loss and latency spikes can cause dropped frames and buffering during live streams.

⚡ Leave Upload Headroom

Never stream using 100% of your upload bandwidth. Keep at least 20–30% available for game traffic, voice chat and network stability.

🎤 Improve Audio Quality

Use 48 kHz sample rate, AAC audio at 160–320 Kbps and enable microphone noise suppression for a cleaner stream.

Common Bitrate Mistakes

❌ Bitrate Too High

Using more bitrate than your internet can handle causes dropped frames, buffering and unstable streams.

❌ Wrong Resolution

Streaming at 1080p with a slow connection usually looks worse than a stable 720p stream.

❌ Wrong Encoder

Choose a hardware encoder whenever possible. Using x264 on a weak CPU may reduce game performance.

❌ Ignoring Platform Limits

Each platform has recommended bitrate ranges. Exceeding them rarely improves quality and may reduce viewer compatibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What bitrate should I use for 1080p60?

For most platforms, 6000–10000 Kbps is recommended. Twitch generally recommends up to 6000 Kbps, while YouTube and Kick can benefit from higher bitrates depending on your connection and stream settings.

Should I always use the highest bitrate?

No. Your bitrate should match both your upload speed and the platform's recommendations. Using an unnecessarily high bitrate can cause buffering and dropped frames for you or your viewers.

Does AV1 improve stream quality?

Yes. AV1 delivers better image quality than H.264 at the same bitrate, but it requires supported hardware and platform compatibility.

How much upload speed do I need?

A good rule is to have at least twice the upload speed of your selected bitrate. For example, a 6000 Kbps stream should ideally have at least a 12–15 Mbps upload connection for stability.

Can I stream over Wi-Fi?

You can, but a wired Ethernet connection is strongly recommended for stable streaming. Wi-Fi is more susceptible to interference, latency and packet loss.

Which encoder should I choose?

Use AV1 if your GPU supports it. Otherwise, NVENC H.264 is an excellent choice for NVIDIA GPUs. AMD users should use AMF, while x264 is best reserved for systems with powerful CPUs.
