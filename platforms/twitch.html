Streaming Setup Checklist

✔ Internet

Use a stable wired Ethernet connection. Maintain at least twice your streaming bitrate as upload speed.

✔ Encoder

Use NVENC H.264 whenever possible. Switch to x264 only if you don't have a supported GPU.

✔ Audio

48kHz sample rate, Stereo audio, 192 Kbps AAC and microphone noise suppression enabled.

✔ OBS

Advanced Output Mode, CBR, 6000 Kbps, Keyframe Interval 2 seconds, Max B-Frames 2.

Professional Streaming Tips

🔥 Encoder Performance

Keep GPU usage below 95% to prevent encoder overload and dropped frames.

🎮 Game Performance

Limit in-game FPS if your GPU constantly reaches 100% usage while streaming.

🌐 Network Stability

Always use wired Ethernet instead of Wi-Fi for lower latency and consistent upload speed.

🧹 Clean System

Close unnecessary applications before going live to free CPU, RAM and GPU resources.

Common Streaming Mistakes

❌ Too High Bitrate

Using more than 6000 Kbps on Twitch usually provides little benefit and may reduce compatibility for viewers.

❌ Streaming on Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi can introduce packet loss, bitrate fluctuations and unstable streams.

❌ Ignoring Audio

Clear audio is often more important to viewers than ultra-high video quality.

❌ GPU at 100%

Leave GPU headroom so OBS can render and encode frames smoothly.

Performance Optimization

⚙ Windows

Enable Game Mode, keep GPU drivers updated and use the High Performance power plan.

📺 OBS

Run OBS as Administrator to improve capture compatibility and encoder stability.

🎙 Audio Filters

Apply Noise Suppression, Noise Gate, Compressor and Limiter for professional microphone quality.

📹 Recording

Record to a separate NVMe SSD if recording while streaming to reduce disk bottlenecks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best bitrate for Twitch?

For most creators, 6000 Kbps provides the best balance between quality and viewer compatibility.

Should I use NVENC or x264?

If you own an NVIDIA RTX graphics card, NVENC H.264 is the recommended encoder because it provides excellent quality with minimal CPU usage.

Can I stream in 1440p or 4K?

Most Twitch creators should stream at 1080p60 or 936p60. Higher resolutions provide little benefit on Twitch.

Why am I dropping frames?

Dropped frames are usually caused by unstable internet, overloaded GPU, incorrect bitrate or Wi-Fi connections.

Should I use Wi-Fi?

No. A wired Ethernet connection is strongly recommended for stable live streaming.
