Professional Streaming Tips

🔥 Stream Stability

Keep your bitrate stable instead of constantly increasing it. A stable stream provides a better viewer experience.

🎮 GPU Usage

Keep GPU usage below 95% while gaming. Leave some headroom for OBS encoding.

📡 Internet

Use at least twice your streaming bitrate as available upload speed. Example: 8000 Kbps stream → 16 Mbps upload.

💻 Background Apps

Close unnecessary applications before going live. This improves both FPS and stream stability.

Common Streaming Mistakes

❌ Too High Bitrate

Higher bitrate isn't always better. Use values recommended for your resolution.

❌ Wi-Fi Streaming

Wireless connections can introduce packet loss and instability. Ethernet is strongly recommended.

❌ 100% GPU Usage

OBS needs GPU resources. Maxing out your GPU can cause encoder overload.

❌ Ignoring Audio

Poor microphone quality drives viewers away faster than average video quality. Invest in clean audio.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I stream at 1080p60?

Yes, if your hardware and internet connection can maintain a stable bitrate. Otherwise, 720p60 often provides a smoother viewing experience.

Is AV1 better than H.264?

Yes. AV1 generally delivers better image quality at the same bitrate, but it requires compatible hardware and software support.

What is the best bitrate for YouTube?

For most creators streaming at 1080p60, 6000–9000 Kbps is a solid starting point. Higher resolutions require higher bitrates.

Should I use CBR?

Yes. Constant Bitrate (CBR) is the recommended rate control mode for live streaming on YouTube.

Can I stream over Wi-Fi?

You can, but a wired Ethernet connection is strongly recommended for lower latency and fewer dropped frames.
