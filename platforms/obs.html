What is OBS Studio?

OBS Studio (Open Broadcaster Software) is one of the world's most popular free and open-source applications for live streaming and screen recording.

It supports streaming to YouTube, Twitch, Kick, Facebook Live and many other platforms while offering professional-grade recording, scene management, audio mixing and hardware encoding.

Whether you're a gamer, content creator, educator or business professional, OBS Studio provides powerful tools completely free of charge.

Installing OBS Studio

1️⃣ Download OBS

Download the latest version of OBS Studio from the official website and choose the installer for your operating system.

2️⃣ Install

Run the installer, keep the default settings unless you have specific requirements, and complete the installation.

3️⃣ Launch OBS

Open OBS Studio. On the first launch you'll be greeted by the Auto Configuration Wizard.

4️⃣ Configure

Select whether OBS will be used mainly for streaming or recording. You can always change these settings later.

Understanding OBS Encoders

🟢 NVENC AV1

Best quality at lower bitrates. Recommended for NVIDIA RTX 40 and RTX 50 Series GPUs.

🟢 NVENC H.264

Excellent quality and low CPU usage. Recommended for GTX 16 Series and RTX 20/30 GPUs.

🟢 AMD AMF

Hardware encoder for AMD graphics cards. Modern RX 7000 GPUs also support AV1.

🟢 Intel Quick Sync

Uses Intel integrated graphics to encode video with minimal CPU usage.

🟢 x264

CPU encoder. Great if you have a powerful processor but can reduce game performance on weaker systems.

🟡 HEVC (H.265)

Excellent for recording. Live streaming support is still limited on many platforms.

Why MKV Instead of MP4?

✅ Crash Protection

If OBS crashes while recording, MKV files are usually recoverable. MP4 recordings can become corrupted.

✅ Easy Remux

OBS can instantly convert MKV to MP4 without re-encoding. File → Remux Recordings

✅ Better for Long Recordings

Ideal for long gaming sessions and live event recordings.

⚠ MP4

Only use MP4 if you absolutely need it immediately after recording.

What is Replay Buffer?

Replay Buffer continuously records the last few seconds or minutes of gameplay into memory.

OBS Scenes & Sources

Scenes are collections of sources. Create separate scenes for Starting Soon, Gameplay, BRB, Full Camera and Ending.

🖥 Game Capture

Best source for capturing games. Lowest performance impact and highest compatibility with modern games.

🖥 Display Capture

Captures your entire monitor. Useful for tutorials and presentations but uses more GPU resources.

Troubleshooting Common OBS Problems

⚠ Encoding Overloaded

Lower your output resolution, reduce FPS, use a hardware encoder (NVENC, AMF or Quick Sync), or close unnecessary background applications.

⚠ Dropped Frames

Usually caused by internet issues. Reduce bitrate, use Ethernet instead of Wi-Fi, and verify your upload speed.

⚠ Black Screen

Run OBS as Administrator, update graphics drivers, or switch between Game Capture and Display Capture.

⚠ Audio Delay

Use OBS Advanced Audio Properties to apply Sync Offset until your microphone and webcam are perfectly synchronized.

⚠ High CPU Usage

Use NVENC or AMD AMF instead of x264, lower output resolution, or reduce FPS.

⚠ Stream Looks Blurry

Increase bitrate, use Lanczos downscaling, avoid scaling multiple times, and keep your upload speed stable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OBS Studio free?

Yes. OBS Studio is completely free and open-source with no subscriptions or hidden costs.

Should I use NVENC or x264?

If you have an NVIDIA GPU, NVENC is recommended because it provides excellent quality while reducing CPU usage. Use x264 only if your CPU is powerful and you don't have a suitable hardware encoder.

Is AV1 better than H.264?

Yes. AV1 provides noticeably better image quality at the same bitrate but requires supported hardware and compatible streaming platforms.

Should I record in MKV or MP4?

Record in MKV to protect recordings from corruption if OBS crashes. After recording, use OBS's "Remux Recordings" feature to convert to MP4 if needed.

How much RAM does OBS need?

8 GB is the minimum, but 16 GB or more is recommended for smooth streaming, recording, and multitasking.

Can I stream and record at the same time?

Yes. OBS supports simultaneous streaming and recording. A modern multi-core CPU and hardware encoder are recommended for the best performance.
